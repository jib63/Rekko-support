Privacy Policy — Apple TV

Last updated: June 12, 2026

This page describes how the Rekko app on Apple TV handles your data. It is a companion to the main Rekko privacy policy that covers the iPhone and iPad versions; the two share the same architecture, but the Apple TV surface is intentionally narrower and a few specifics differ. Where this page is silent, the main policy applies.

1. Who is responsible for your data?

Rekko is developed and operated by Jean-Baptiste Meyer ("we", "us"), acting as the data controller under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR / RGPD). Any question about this policy can be sent to jib@jibstudios.com.

2. What data the Apple TV app handles

Rekko on Apple TV is a lean-back, read-mostly companion to the iPhone app. It shows you what is already in your iCloud Rekko data; it does not create new categories of personal data on its own. Concretely it handles:

  • Sign-in identifier. When you sign in with Sign in with Apple on Apple TV, Apple gives Rekko a stable, opaque userIdentifier. We do not store your real Apple ID, your name, or your email address. The email Apple offers to share is intentionally discarded. The identifier is kept in the Apple TV's local keychain.
  • Your profile, journal and circles — read from iCloud. Display name, avatar thumbnail, followed shows and viewing statuses, ratings, circle memberships and friends' recommendations are read from your existing iCloud Rekko zones (see §3). The Apple TV app does not let you edit your profile, your avatar, your favourite genres, your ratings or your circle members — those surfaces stay on iPhone. Apple TV is read-only for personal data; only the followed status of a series can be flipped from the detail page.
  • Anonymous device attestation. Rekko uses Apple's App Attest framework to prove that requests to its read-only relay come from a legitimate copy of the app. App Attest does not expose any personal information.

Rekko on Apple TV does not collect: your contacts, location, microphone, advertising identifier, or any third-party analytics. There is no in-app tracking SDK.

3. Where your data is stored

Same architecture as on iPhone: personal data is stored in your own private iCloud space, via Apple's CloudKit framework. The Apple TV app reads the same iCloud Rekko zones the iPhone app writes to.

  • Your profile, your journal, and your locally-owned circles live in your iCloud Private Database, accessible only by your Apple ID.
  • Shared circles use Apple's CKShare mechanism: data is replicated only to the iCloud accounts you have explicitly invited. Apple TV reads these shared zones via the same mechanism the iPhone does.
  • A read-only relay we operate is used to securely sign requests to TMDB (see §4). It does not store any of your data and only logs short-lived technical traces for abuse prevention.

4. Third parties involved

  • Apple Inc. processes your iCloud data on its servers, under Apple's Privacy Policy. The CloudKit infrastructure that hosts your circles, profile and journal is Apple's, not ours.
  • The Movie Database (TMDB). Rekko fetches TV series metadata (titles, posters, synopses, episode info, ratings) from TMDB. The TMDB request includes only the identifier of the series you are looking at; no personal Rekko identifier is sent. TMDB is the source of the catalogue and is not affiliated with Rekko.
  • The Apple TV app (built-in). When you tap "Where to watch", Rekko opens the system Apple TV app at a search URL containing only the series title. Rekko does not see which streaming service the Apple TV app then sends you to, and does not record the hand-off.

5. Permissions Rekko on Apple TV may ask for

  • iCloud. Required: without an iCloud account signed in on the Apple TV, the app cannot read your data or honour circle invitations. This is exactly the same requirement as on iPhone.
  • Sign in with Apple. Required to bind the device to your Rekko profile.

The Apple TV app does not request access to: Photos, Notifications, Contacts, Location, Microphone, Camera, Bluetooth, or any other device sensor. Profile photo selection, push notifications, and the Photos picker live exclusively on iPhone.

6. What the Apple TV app does NOT do

To make the read-only nature explicit:

  • No purchases on Apple TV. The Founders pact and Premium subscription cannot be bought from the Apple TV app. If you want to support Rekko or upgrade, do it from the iPhone. The Apple TV app only reads the entitlement state that was set on iPhone (e.g. to show a golden ring around your Founders avatar).
  • No notifications on Apple TV. Streaming reminders and circle activity notifications are an iPhone-only feature.
  • No profile editing on Apple TV. Display name, avatar, favourite genres are all set on iPhone.
  • No movies. By product decision, the Apple TV app is series-only — your iPhone-tracked movies do not appear, and you cannot follow new movies from Apple TV.

7. Legal basis

All processing of personal data is based on your consent (GDPR art. 6(1)(a)) — given when you sign in on Apple TV — and on the performance of a contract (art. 6(1)(b)) for the functionality you actively request (opening a circle's detail, flipping the followed status of a series).

8. How long we keep your data

For as long as your account exists. Deleting your account from the iPhone Rekko app wipes your CloudKit data; the Apple TV app then has nothing to read and the bookmark disappears. From the Apple TV app itself you can sign out via Settings, which clears the sign-in identifier from the Apple TV's local keychain — your data on iCloud is untouched, which means you can re-sign in on any device and pick up where you left off.

A full account deletion (and the matching CloudKit wipe + App Attest revocation) is the same operation on both surfaces and can be triggered from either: iPhone Settings → Delete my account, or Apple TV Settings → Delete all data.

9. Your rights under GDPR

Your rights are identical to the iPhone surface — see §7 of the main privacy policy for the full list (access, portability, rectification, erasure, objection, withdrawal of consent, lodging a complaint with the CNIL). One small implementation detail: the "Export my data" action lives on iPhone only, because that is where the file output goes. Triggering it on iPhone produces a JSON file covering every Rekko surface, including everything the Apple TV app would otherwise show you.

10. Children

Rekko is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has signed up, please contact us and we will delete the corresponding account.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when the app or applicable law change. We will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page, and a meaningful change will be surfaced in-app the next time you open Settings on either iPhone or Apple TV.
